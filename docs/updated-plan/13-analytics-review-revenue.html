What is "LTV per customer"?

Lifetime value (LTV) is the total revenue you expect to earn from one customer across all the visits they'll make over a 12-month window — not just their first visit. The ticket revenue above shows what BAAM Review earned you this month. LTV shows what those same customers will be worth across the year.

Acupuncture example: $150 ticket × ~6.5 visits/year ≈ $980 LTV

Show assumptions behind the numbers
  • Avg ticket — your input. Drives the "Revenue this period" number on the left of the hero.
  • LTV per customer — your input. Drives the "Lifetime value" number on the right of the hero. Equals avg ticket × visits/year. Use a 12-month window.
  • Close rate (50%) — your input. Not every friend who clicks Book actually converts; 50% is a conservative default.
  • Profile-view → booking (1%) — fixed at 1%. BrightLocal 2024 shows 1–3% for local services; we use the floor.
  • Review attribution share (50%) — your input. Profile-view lift comes from many things (ads, new content, seasonality); we credit half to new reviews by default.
  • Profile views — pulled from your Google Business Profile Performance API, period-over-period.
  • Conservative / optimistic range — ±35% applied only to the modeled (review) bucket.
  • What we deliberately don't count — ranking lift, brand equity, widget impressions. Too speculative to defend.

Numbers update nightly. Sources: Google Business Profile Performance API, BrightLocal Local Consumer Review Survey 2024.
